Local Specialties

Must-Try Local Specialties

From famous Craster kippers to traditional stottie cakes, discover the authentic tastes that make Northumberland special

Craster Kippers

These famous smoked herrings are a true taste of the Northumberland coast. Split and brined, then cold-smoked, they offer a distinctive flavor. Enjoy them on their own or in a traditional Kedgeree.

Where to Find Them:

  • L. Robson & Sons: The traditional smokehouse in Craster
  • Local fishmongers and restaurants along the Northumberland coast

Lindisfarne Mead

Experience a taste of Holy Island's history with this fortified mead, blending honey with grapes. Enjoy it dry, sweet, or as a spiced version for a warming treat.

Where to Find Them:

  • St Aidan's Winery: Located on Holy Island, where it's produced
  • Speciality food shops across Northumberland

Northumbrian Cheeses

Explore a variety of artisanal cheeses crafted by local producers in Northumberland. Look out for award-winning options like Berwick Edge and Cuddy's Cave from Doddington Dairy.

Where to Find Them:

  • Doddington Dairy Milk Bar & Cafe: In Wooler, offers cheese and ice cream
  • Independent Food & Drink: In Seahouses, boasts a dedicated cheese counter
  • Alnwick Farmers' Market: On the last Friday of each month

Northumbrian Meats & Game

From succulent hill lamb to flavorful beef and game birds, Northumberland boasts a rich tradition of high-quality meat production.

Where to Find Them:

  • Turnbull's Northumbrian Food Hall: In Alnwick, offers a selection of locally sourced meats
  • North Acomb Farm Shop: Known for its farm-raised beef, lamb, and pork

Fresh Seafood

With its long coastline, Northumberland offers an abundance of fresh seafood. Indulge in everything from turbot and oysters to crab sandwiches and lobster.

Where to Find Them:

  • The Potted Lobster: In Bamburgh, known for dishes using locally caught seafood
  • The Jolly Fisherman: In Craster, famed for its crab soup and sea views
  • Northumberland Seafood Centre: In Amble, champions sustainable fishing

Northumbrian Stottie Cake

A dense, round flatbread with an indent in the center. Traditionally halved and filled to make a hearty sandwich. A local favorite pairing is with ham and pease pudding.

Where to Find Them:

  • Many local bakeries and cafes across Northumberland

Pease Pudding

A thick, smooth paste made from split yellow peas, often served with ham, gammon, or sausages. It can also accompany fish and chips or be spread on a stottie.

Where to Find Them:

  • Many traditional eateries and cafes across the region
  • Found alongside other local produce at farmers' markets and food shops

Singin' Hinnies

These traditional griddle scones are rich with butter, currants, and make a "singing" sound as they cook. Enjoy them warm with a cup of tea.

Where to Find Them:

  • Many local cafes and bakeries in Northumberland
  • Some holiday accommodations may even offer them for guests to try

Northumbrian Ice Cream

Indulge in delicious ice cream made on local dairy farms in Northumberland.

Where to Find Them:

  • Doddington Dairy Milk Bar & Cafe: In Wooler
  • Morwick Dairy: A popular spot for ice cream

Pan Haggerty

This hearty Northumbrian dish is a variation of dauphinoise potatoes, made with thinly sliced potatoes, mature cheddar cheese, and fried onions. Enjoy it as a warming meal or as a side dish.

Where to Find Them:

  • Restaurants and pubs featuring traditional Northumberland cuisine

Explore More Culinary Delights

Beyond these specialties, Northumberland's food scene also offers:

Locally brewed ales and spirits
Artisan breads from historic water mills
Heritage potatoes and asparagus
Award-winning pies

Look out for establishments marked with the "Produced in Northumberland" stamp, which verifies that the food you're enjoying has come straight from the county.
