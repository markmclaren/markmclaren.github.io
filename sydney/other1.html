Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Friday, April 18th - Arrival in Sydney

5:20 PM: Arrive at Sydney Airport (SYD). Pick up rental car. Check into your hotel (city center).

Evening: Relax and have dinner near your hotel. Consider a casual pub meal.

Day 2: Saturday, April 19th - Exploring Sydney

Morning: Self-guided walking tour of The Rocks (historic district).

Lunch: The Rocks - many pubs and restaurants.

Afternoon: Sydney Opera House tour & walk around Circular Quay.

Evening: Dinner in Circular Quay.

Day 3: Sunday, April 20th - Aussie Rules & Cats

Morning: Check out of hotel, travel to Matraville (granny annex).

Afternoon: Attend the Sydney Swans game!

Evening: Dinner in Matraville or nearby. Optional: Cat cafe visit (check hours).

Day 4: Monday, April 21st - Great Pacific Drive to Kiama

Morning: Start the Grand Pacific Drive.

Stop 1: Royal National Park (short bushwalk).

Lunch: Picnic or cafe in a coastal town.

Stop 2: Sea Cliff Bridge (photo stop).

Afternoon/Evening: Arrive in Kiama, check in, dinner.

Day 5: Tuesday, April 22nd - Kiama Exploration

Morning: Kiama Blowhole (check tide times), Kiama Coast Walk.

Lunch: Kiama cafes/restaurants.

Afternoon: Explore Kiama Harbour (boat tour optional).

Evening: Dinner in Kiama.

Day 6: Wednesday, April 23rd - Drive to Blue Mountains

Morning: Breakfast in Kiama, depart for the Blue Mountains.

Afternoon: Arrive in Blue Mountains (Katoomba or Leura), check in.

Evening: Dinner in the Blue Mountains.

Day 7: Thursday, April 24th - Blue Mountains & Return to Sydney

Morning: Scenic walk (Three Sisters, Wentworth Falls, or Leura Cascades).

Lunch: Katoomba or Leura.

Afternoon: Scenic World (optional).

Late Afternoon/Evening: Drive back to Matraville, Sydney. Dinner.

Day 8: Friday, April 25th - Anzac Day

Anzac Day Observance: Dawn Service (Cenotaph, Martin Place), Anzac Day March.

Afternoon: Reflect, visit Australian War Memorial (Hyde Park) (optional).

Evening: Quiet dinner.

Day 9: Saturday, April 26th - Sydney Exploration

Morning: Taronga Zoo Sydney.

Lunch: Zoo or city.

Afternoon: Hyde Park & Museums (Australian Museum or Art Gallery of NSW).

Evening: Dinner in Surry Hills or Newtown.

Day 10: Sunday, April 27th - Family/Friend Visit (Flexible)

Flexible day for your visit.

Restaurant Suggestion: Darling Harbour.

Day 11: Monday, April 28th - Family/Friend Visit (Flexible)

Flexible day for your visit.

Activity Suggestion: Ferry trip to Manly.

Day 12: Tuesday, April 29th - Sydney Harbour Boat Trip

Morning/Afternoon: Sydney Harbour boat trip (ferry or organized cruise).

Evening: Dinner in a waterfront restaurant.

Day 13: Wednesday, April 30th - Hidden Gems & Film

Morning: Explore Sydney's neighborhoods (Paddington, Glebe, Newtown).

Lunch: In chosen suburb.

Afternoon/Evening: Film (independent cinema or 'Colin from Accounts' locations).

Dinner: Choose based on cuisine preference.

Day 14: Thursday, May 1st - Departure

Early Morning: Depart from Sydney Airport (SYD) at 6:00 AM.

Return rental car.

Restaurant Recommendations

Here are some restaurant suggestions, keeping in mind your dietary needs (vegetarian, fish, fried chicken/steak). It's always a good idea to check the latest menus and reviews online.

Sydney

  • Yellow (Potts Point): Upscale vegetarian fine dining.

    Pricey, but excellent for a special occasion.

  • Bodhi in the Park (CBD): Vegan Yum Cha.

    Great for lunch.

  • Spice Alley (Chippendale): Various Asian cuisines.

    Vibrant, many options.

  • Sean's Panaroma (Bondi): Fresh seafood, vegetarian options.

    Beautiful Bondi setting.

  • Mary's (Various Locations): Burgers, fried chicken.

    Casual, good for son's preference.

  • Italian Restaurants (Leichhardt): Many trattorias/osterias.

    Search in "Little Italy" for pasta, seafood.

Kiama

  • The Hungry Monkey: Burgers (including vegetarian).

    Casual, good for a quick bite.

  • Hanoi on Manning: Vietnamese with vegetarian/seafood.

    Fresh and flavorful.

  • Check local cafes/restaurants for fresh seafood and vegetarian pasta/salads.

Blue Mountains

  • Leura Garage (Leura): Modern Australian cuisine.

    Good for variety.

  • Silk's Brasserie (Katoomba): Fine dining, local produce.

    For a more upscale experience.

Activities by Interest

Here are activities tailored to your interests:

Cats

Search online for "cat cafe Sydney" for current options. Check local animal shelters for adoption events.

Historical Sites

  • Sydney: The Rocks, Hyde Park Barracks Museum, Vaucluse House, Susannah Place Museum, Cockatoo Island.
  • Great Pacific Drive: Wollongong Head Lighthouse, Kiama Pilot's Cottage Museum.
  • Blue Mountains: Historic towns of Katoomba and Leura.

TV and Films

  • Cinemas: Hayden Orpheum Picture Palace (Cremorne), Dendy Cinemas, Palace Cinemas.
  • 'Colin from Accounts': Search for filming location guides (inner west).
  • Studio Ghibli: Check cinema listings for screenings.

Hidden Gems

Explore neighborhoods (Paddington, Glebe, Newtown, Surry Hills, Chippendale), markets, ferry trips to less-visited suburbs, small bars/cafes.

Australian Foods

Try modern Australian cuisine, seafood, vegetarian dishes with native vegetables, pub food. Sample Vegemite, Lamingtons, Pavlova, Tim Tams.

Australian Wildlife

  • Zoos/Sanctuaries: Taronga Zoo Sydney, Featherdale Sydney Wildlife Park, Symbio Wildlife Park, Australian Reptile Park. (Consider ethical views on zoos).
  • Natural Viewing: Royal National Park (bushwalking), beaches.

Walking Tours

  • Self-Guided: The Rocks Walking Tour (free), city council websites.
  • Guided: Search online for walking tour companies.

Culture

  • Aboriginal Culture: The Rocks Aboriginal Dreaming Tour, Art Gallery of NSW, Australian Museum.
  • Museums: Australian Museum, Art Gallery of NSW, Museum of Sydney.
  • Festivals: Check event listings during your visit.

Transportation

You'll have a rental car for the entire trip, which is ideal for the Great Pacific Drive and exploring beyond the city center.

Driving Tips

  • Drive on the left side of the road!
  • Be aware of speed limits and road rules.
  • Parking can be challenging and expensive in the city center. Consider parking at your accommodation or using park-and-ride facilities.
  • The Grand Pacific Drive is a scenic route; take your time and enjoy the views.

Public Transport in Sydney

Sydney has a good public transport system (trains, buses, ferries, light rail). An Opal card is used for fares.
