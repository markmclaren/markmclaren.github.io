Write them down so you can see them clearly. Tick the ones that applied to you, and add your own thoughts below.

02

Acknowledge the new factors honestly

These might be influencing you now. Tick any that feel true — there's no wrong answer here.

None of these make you weak or irrational. They're normal reactions to a big transition. The key is making sure they don't drown out the long-term picture.
03

Think about exam results realistically

It's easy to assume the worst before results come out. But assumptions aren't facts.

Answer these reflection questions honestly. Tap each one to reveal a prompt.

Results day hasn't happened yet. Any decision made now is based on a prediction — and predictions are often more pessimistic than reality. It might be worth waiting before making any changes.

Imagine opening your results and they're exactly what you hoped for. How would you feel if you'd already switched to an alternative option? Would you wish you'd waited?

Many unis have clearing, foundation years, or alternative entry routes. Lower results don't always mean the door is closed — it's worth checking before assuming.

Staying local might feel safer, but it doesn't change your results. It just removes one unknown. Is that trade-off worth it for your long-term goals?

04

Clear the fog — imagine a clean slate

This isn't about denying your relationship or your worries. It's just a way to see past the noise.

If you were single AND confident in your results — which uni would you choose?

That answer often reveals what your long-term self actually wants. Write it down below.

07

Look at the relationship realistically but positively

Your relationship matters. It's okay that it's part of your thinking. But it shouldn't be the only part.

Closeness doesn't save weak ones.

If it isn't, staying local won't fix that.

You don't have to choose between your future and your relationship.

09

The final question

Before you decide, consider these three scenarios honestly.

If you choose the original uni and your relationship is strong — it will still be there.

If you choose the original uni and the relationship doesn't last — you still have the opportunities you earned.

If you choose the alternative option and it's genuinely the better fit — you'll have chosen it with a clear head, not by default.

If you choose the alternative option and the relationship changes anyway — you'll still have learned what mattered most to you.

Whichever way you're leaning — is it because of genuine preference, or because of fear?

10
